# Tapeline — Newsletter Sponsorship Research

Drafted 2026-05-14. 5 finance newsletters where Tapeline's audience fit + the price point work for a first sponsorship test. Budget assumption: $300-1,200 single-newsletter buy or 2 × $300-500 small-newsletter buys. The brief said $300; spend may need to flex up to land a single placement with the right audience.

**Goal**: validate whether newsletter audiences convert at the unit economics Tapeline needs. Target metric: **trial signups per $100 sponsorship spend ≥ 3**. Anything below 1 says newsletters aren't the right channel for the current price point and you should redirect that budget to Twitter/Reddit organic.

**Realistic outcome**: 1 of the 5 newsletters lands at the budget. The big two (Daily Upside, Finimize) are likely $$$$ — you'll learn their rate cards as part of this exercise. The Substack tier is more accessible and where the unit economics likely work.

**Important — finance newsletter sponsorship CPMs in 2026**:
> "Finance, investing, and wealth management newsletters command $40-$80 CPM (cost per 1,000 opens) for primary placements." — InfluencersKit 2026 rate card guide

A $300 budget at $50 CPM = ~6,000 opens worth of exposure. That's tight for a newsletter with > 50K subscribers; comfortable for the 5K-30K Substack tier.

## What to do once a rate comes back

For each accepted rate, run the unit-economics check **before signing**:

```
Estimated reach = subscribers × open_rate × CTR
Cost per click  = sponsorship cost / Estimated reach
Conversion rate = unknown but estimate 3-7% trial-from-click on a fit-audience newsletter
Cost per trial  = cost per click / conversion rate
Cost per paid   = cost per trial × estimated trial-to-paid (~10%)
```

**Decision rule**: only commit if estimated cost per paid customer < $35 (Pro tier monthly first-month revenue). If a $1,500 Net Interest slot at 90K opens and 2% CTR gets 1,800 clicks → 90 trials at 5% → 9 paid at 10% → $167/paid acquisition cost. That FAILS the rule; pass on it OR negotiate the rate down OR test with a smaller slot first.

The math will likely show that ONLY the Substack tier ($300-$1,500 range) is sustainable on Tapeline's unit economics. The big newsletters will fail the math — and that's a valid signal to redirect that budget into Twitter/Reddit organic where the CAC is effectively zero.

## What to do if all 5 rates fail unit economics

Drop the channel for now and focus the time on Twitter (`@tapeline_io` daily posts) + Reddit (8-week calendar at `docs/growth/reddit_calendar.md`). Newsletters become a re-test in Q4 2026 after Tapeline's free-tier audience exists and you have a referral mechanic generating organic conversions (already shipped — Channel 5 / PR #22).

## UTM tags

Every newsletter URL must include:
```
?utm_source=newsletter&utm_campaign=<newsletter_short>&utm_content=<slot_type>
```

Mapping: `dailyupside`, `finimize`, `netinterest`, `bearcave`, `financenews`. Slot type: `primary`, `secondary`, `classifieds`, `footer`.

Vercel Analytics segments these automatically; the per-newsletter conversion rate gets visible in the dashboard a week after the placement runs.
